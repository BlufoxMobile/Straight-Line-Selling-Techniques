Building Rapport & Earning Trust

Every Sale in History Happened
for the Exact Same Reason

You wouldn't buy shoes you thought were fake, a car from a liar, or jewelry from a shady jeweler. Trust is the building block for every type of sale — and Belfort breaks the buyer's decision into three things they must trust before they buy.

The Straight Line System

The sale is a straight line from the open to the close. Your job is to keep the customer on the line, moving toward the close — while staying within the boundaries of the conversation.

The Secret in the Sauce

The 1 and the 10 don't need you — a 1 isn't interested and a 10 is already in love. The magic is the 5s through 9s: they're not a yes, but they're not a no either. 05
The Mobile Script

The 4 Questions That
Open the Door

iPad in hand, filling out the t-sheet, follow the path. Tap each card to flip it and see exactly why every word matters. Remember: act like whatever they say is exactly what you hoped to hear.

QUESTION 1
"There's another discount you qualify for — our mobile discount. Who do you have your cell phones with?"
↻ tap to flip

WHY IT WORKS

Lead with the keywords DISCOUNT and QUALIFY. "Discount" makes it feel like you're helping, not selling — a customer-service pitch, not a sales pitch.

"Qualify" is a strong word: customers always qualify for mobile if they have internet. It means they qualify to sign up for lines — not to finance a phone. That's all we're pitching here.

QUESTION 2
"Perfect! How many lines do you have?"
↻ tap to flip

WHY IT WORKS

Whatever they say is "Perfect!" Act as if it's exactly what you were hoping to hear, every time.

Never speak negatively about other companies. Never downplay their decisions. You're gathering facts to build the offer.

QUESTION 3
"Great! How much does your cell phone bill cost you each month?"
↻ tap to flip

WHY IT WORKS

Again — "Great!" no matter the answer. This uncovers a pain point by implying something without saying it.

Asking it this way gets the actual number, instead of "too much" or "I don't know."

QUESTION 4 — the big one
"Why are you paying so much???"
↻ tap to flip

THE MOST IMPORTANT QUESTION

Their answer is the objection you're about to hear when you present. It tells you what they know about their current plan.

If they owe on the phone, they'll tell you. If they just bought it, they'll tell you. And if they don't know why they're paying so much — they'd better buy some phones from you!

Stay disciplined. Don't jump the gun. Follow the script to the next step no matter what.

The Mobile Presentation

iPad in hand, filling the t-sheet, for two reasons: it establishes consistent behavior in every transaction, and it controls speed — rushing kills deals, but so does dead air that loses engagement. Get as many NO's as you can in a day. The more you present, the more you hear NO — and the more NO's, the more YES's. 📄 Digital T-Sheet

Common Pitfalls to Avoid — the 80/20 rule: don't over-talk

1. Never speak negatively about a customer's buying decision.No downplaying what they chose before.
2. Don't say "we use Verizon's towers."Build value instead: "we're partnered with Verizon."
3. Don't ask if their phone is paid off.Don't hand them an objection to throw back at you.
4. Don't recommend objections for the customer.Sometimes great selling is about what you don't say.
5. Don't pitch financed devices until you have to.And only once you know exactly what they qualify for.
6. Don't drown them in options.Too many choices = no decision today. Keep it clean.

Existing Mobile Customers = Pre-Closed

These are your best customers on the scale of certainty — they're walking in pre-closed. They already said yes to Xfinity internet, yes to Xfinity Mobile, and now they've talked themselves into buying a new phone. Fact-find every asset: their current phone, what they're qualified for, membership status, current phone bill, and core bill. Done right, you give them more value than they came in for AND put yourself in position to triple, quadruple, or 5X your commission. 📈 Upgrade Quote Sheet

06
Closing the Sale

You'll Hear NO 7 Times
Before You Hear YES

Overcoming objections is how you take people who were a 5–9 when they walked in and move them to a closed sale. Our proven, duplicatable process averages 1 close per 5 presentations — so present often. And when you address an objection, looping is what makes the difference.

1 Address the objection
→
2 Summarize the offer
→
3 Present your close again
↻
↺ Loop until YES

The 4 Most Common Objections — tap to handle

"I'll think about it.
There's nothing to think about that we can't cover right now. Address what's really behind it, summarize the value they'd walk away with today, and present the close once more.
▾ TAP TO HANDLE
"I don't have time.
Respect their time — that's exactly why the t-sheet and iPad keep it fast. Re-summarize the offer in one breath and ask for the close. Speed and discipline win here.
▾ TAP TO HANDLE
"I need to talk to my spouse.
Acknowledge it, then loop: reinforce the value and savings, remind them the offer is on the table now, and present the close. Make staying easy and leaving feel like missing out.
▾ TAP TO HANDLE
"I'm paying off a device / in a contract.
This is why you asked Question 4 first — you already know the pain. Address it head-on, show how switching still wins, summarize, and close. Don't let it derail the line.
▾ TAP TO HANDLE
